Hyderabad

Hyderabad, the capital of Telangana, is a major technological and cultural hub in India. Founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the city has a rich historical background, blending Persian, Mughal, and South Indian influences. One of its most iconic landmarks is the Charminar, which was built in the same year as the cityâ€™s establishment. Hyderabad was once ruled by the Nizams, who made it one of the richest princely states in India. Their legacy is still reflected in the city's architecture, traditions, and cultural heritage. Over the years, Hyderabad has transformed into a modern IT hub, often referred to as "Cyberabad." The city is home to HITEC City (Hyderabad Information Technology and Engineering Consultancy City), where major tech giants like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon have their offices. The city's economic growth is also fueled by its strong presence in industries like pharmaceuticals and film production. Ramoji Film City, the world's largest film studio complex, attracts filmmakers and tourists alike. Hyderabad is world-famous for its Hyderabadi Biryani, a delicious blend of Mughlai and Telugu flavors. Another historical attraction is the Golconda Fort, which was once a global diamond-trading hub.
